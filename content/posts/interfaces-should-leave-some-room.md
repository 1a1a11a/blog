The best tools do not disappear. They become quiet enough that we can hear our own intentions while using them.

Modern software often promises to remove every pause. It predicts the next word, selects the next destination, and turns any repeated gesture into an automated path. Convenience is useful, but a life without pauses is not necessarily a life with more agency.

## Friction can carry information

A small moment of resistance asks us to decide. The blank page asks what matters. A confirmation step asks whether we meant what we said. A map that does not reroute instantly lets us understand where we are.

> Good friction is not an obstacle placed in the way. It is a surface that gives the hand something to feel.

This is not an argument for cumbersome software. It is an argument for distinguishing effort that wastes attention from effort that focuses it.

## Leave the last move to the person

Design can suggest, arrange, and clarify. But the final act should still feel like it belongs to the person at the keyboard. The interface should leave enough room for surprise, revision, and a change of mind.

That room is where judgment lives.
